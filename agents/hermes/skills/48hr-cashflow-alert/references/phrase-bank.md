# Phrase Bank — Alert Writing

**Purpose:** Rotate phrases to avoid robotic repetition. Pick contextually, don't use the same phrase twice in a row across alerts.

---

## Win Celebrations (Close Alerts)

Use after profitable exits:

- "Hell of a trade."
- "That's how it's done."
- "Nice work."
- "Clean win."
- "Nailed it."
- "That's the game."
- "This is why we do this."
- "Textbook execution."
- "You saw it, you took it, you banked it."

---

## Closing Lines

End-of-alert momentum:

- "Onto the next."
- "Next one's loading."
- "More coming."
- "Stay ready."
- "Back soon with another setup."
- "Keep your powder dry."

---

## Pattern Validation

When the pattern did its job:

- "Bull Surge did its job."
- "Pattern fired, you followed, it paid."
- "This is why we trust the setup."
- "The setup worked exactly as designed."
- "Same pattern, same result."
- "[Pattern name] keeps printing."

---

## Entry Confidence

Why we're taking this trade:

- "The pattern is clear."
- "Setup's here. Time to move."
- "This is what we wait for."
- "Stars aligned on this one."
- "Everything lines up."

---

## Risk Reminders

Keep it real, not scary:

- "Manage your size."
- "Only risk what you can lose."
- "Position accordingly."
- "Respect the stop."
- "No trade is a sure thing."

---

## Stop-Out / Loss Acknowledgment

When trades don't work:

- "This one didn't work. It happens."
- "Stop hit. We move on."
- "Not every setup wins. That's trading."
- "Loss taken. Risk managed."
- "We followed the rules. Sometimes you lose."
- "Part of the game."

---

## Urgency (Without Hype)

When timing matters, no pressure tactics:

- "This is moving. Act if you're in."
- "Window's open."
- "Setup is live."
- "Clock's ticking on this one."

---

## Usage Notes

1. **Rotate** — Don't use same phrase in consecutive alerts
2. **Match context** — Big win = bigger celebration. Small win = understated
3. **Service voice** — Hot Money is more investigative, 48hr more methodical
4. **Read aloud** — If it sounds like a robot, swap it
