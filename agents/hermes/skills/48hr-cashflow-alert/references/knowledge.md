### Narrative Arc for BUY Alerts (from gold standards)

Same arc as HMT but different voice — calm, insurance-minded, probability-driven.

**OPENING** — Match the situation. Each alert opens differently:
- Earnings: "[TICKER] reports after the close today. $X expected move on a $X stock."
- Post-crash: "[TICKER] is down X% in X days. The options market is bracing for another X% drop."
- Post-rally: "[TICKER] just ripped X% in one session. That creates mispricing."
- Mystery: "I can't find what they're bracing for."

**SYSTEM SIGNAL** — "Our system flagged the puts as overpriced. We're selling that fear/premium."

**RETURN LINE** — "If they expire worthless, we collect X%." or "And if we're right, that's X% by Friday at 4 PM."

**"The 48-Hour Window Just Opened"** — Section heading. Stock chart follows.

**COMPANY CONTEXT** — 1-2 sentences. What they do + why it matters for THIS trade.

**PRICE + EXPECTED MOVE** — "Right now [TICKER] is at $X. The options market is pricing in a $X move by [day]."

**THE SETUP** — Why the fear is overpriced. This is the research layer. Specific, non-obvious catalysts:
- AXTI: China export permits + semiconductor selloff + stock already down 34%
- RCAT: Pre-announced revenue (1,800% growth), last quarter's miss was the anchor fear
- FIG: Adobe acquisition blocked, stock already lost 80% of value
- PATH: CEO resigned last year + guidance cuts, but last quarter was strongest in 2 years

**ACTION** — Clean. No "Capital Required", no "Max Loss", no "Entry: $X credit minimum."
```
ACTION: Sell-to-Open [TICKER] [EXP] $[HIGH] Put / Buy the $[LOW] Put at $[CREDIT] credit
• Premium Collected: $X per spread
• Return: X% if [TICKER] stays above $[HIGH] through [day]
```

**RISK** — Woven into narrative. NOT a labeled section. One paragraph naming the specific event that could break the trade.

**CLOSING THESIS** — "X% return for 48 hours of patience and probabilities."

**"What This Trade Looks Like"** — Section heading. Options analysis chart.

### Win Alert Arc
- OPENER: Result first. "Another one. 25% in 48 hours." or "Good news. We're back on the winning side."
- WHAT HAPPENED: Earnings result or stock action that proved the thesis
- CURRENT SITUATION: Price now, where strikes are
- THE MATH: "$X on $X risk. X% in 48 hours."
- "No action needed." — Both puts expire worthless. Broker handles it.
- RECORD: "**48-Hour Cashflow Record: X-Y (Z% win rate)**" + Trade Tracker link
- CLOSER: "Back Wednesday." (or "Back next Wednesday" if Friday close)

- CURRENT PRICE: Below the strike
- NET LOSS: "We collected $X but lost the full $Y risk." ONE LINE. No bullet breakdown.
- INSURANCE FRAMING: "This is why we don't sell naked options. We hedge every time."
- "No action needed. Your broker handles it automatically."
- CONTEXT: Brief — "one loss doesn't break a winning system" or "tough market this week"
- RECORD + Trade Tracker link
- CLOSER: "Back [next] Wednesday with a fresh setup."

## What Makes 48HC Alerts Different from HMT/OI

1. **Calm, not aggressive.** HMT is conviction-driven ("That's a signal worth following"). 48HC is probability-driven ("25% return for 48 hours of patience and probabilities").
2. **Insurance framing.** "Like State Farm paying a claim." Loss alerts explicitly reference the hedge structure.
3. **The thesis is always: fear is overpriced.** Not "the stock is going up" but "the stock isn't going to crash as much as the options market thinks."
4. **Expected move matters.** Include it when Josh provides it — "$X expected move on a $X stock."
5. **Risk is specific and honest.** Name the exact event that breaks the trade. "If earnings blow up, the stock can move way past where the options market expected."
6. **Record is mandatory on update alerts.** Both wins and losses include the running record.
