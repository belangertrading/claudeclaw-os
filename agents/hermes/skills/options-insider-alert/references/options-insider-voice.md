# Options Insider Voice & Examples

Updated: 2026-02-20 — Rebuilt from actual published alerts

## Service Identity

**Options Insider is a momentum-triggered pattern service.** Language must reflect this. Use momentum framing, not thesis/analysis framing.
- ✅ "Bullish momentum killed before Monday"
- ✅ "The momentum reversed overnight"
- ❌ "Thesis dead" — sounds like fundamental analysis, not a momentum service
- ❌ "Our analysis was wrong" — we follow patterns, not theses

## Voice Rules

**Pattern-focused, systematic:**
- Reference historical pattern performance (win rate, total returns)
- Custom bold section header — NOT "The Fundamentals Support the Pattern" (see below)
- Direct, punchy sentences. First-person voice. "I'm not missing this one twice."
- End with: "Get in, get the [X]%, get out."

**Opening — flexible, not a formula:**
No single template. Each alert should find its own voice based on the specific setup. The opener should create tension, contrast, or surprise — not just announce the ticker.

Some openers lead with the market being wrong: "Everyone's betting against Tesla. The Bull Surge says they're wrong."
Some lead with the pattern itself: "This coal stock just triggered our 719% pattern — for the second time."
Some lead with the event: "The Supreme Court just handed Vegas a free pass."

The ticker is in the headline, so the opener doesn't need to restate it. Find the most interesting angle for THIS specific trade and lead with that. Don't copy openers from other alerts — variety is the point.

**The Fundamentals Section:**
- Header is a CUSTOM BOLD HEADLINE that tells the specific story in one line
- NOT "The Fundamentals Support the Pattern" — that's generic and wrong
- Examples: "Musk Just Killed Two Cars to Build Robots" / "Coal to Gas — The Market Doesn't Buy It"
- 3-4 SHORT PARAGRAPHS, NO bullets, flowing prose, punchy sentences
- Find the ONE narrative that explains why the pattern is firing NOW
- Specific facts only — dollar amounts, dates, percentages, names

**Spread Alternative (optional but common):**
- After ACTION, offer a spread variation to reduce cost/risk
- Format: "Want to maximize return and reduce cost? You can turn this into a [call/put] spread by selling the [TICKER] [EXP] $[STRIKE] [Call/Put] against it."

**Never include:**
- Em dashes ( — ) anywhere. Use periods, commas, or restructure the sentence. No exceptions.
- Technical analysis (support, resistance, moving averages) — pattern handles that
- Invented facts — use only verified research
- Generic opener like "[TICKER] is about to rip higher" — too flat
- "The Fundamentals Support the Pattern" as a section header

## Patterns & Management Rules

| Pattern | Take Profit | Exit Rule |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **Bull Surge** | 40% gain | 60% loss |
| **Bear Plunge** | 40% gain | 60% loss |
| **U-Turn** | 50% gain | Exit day of expiration if TP not hit |
| **U-Turn Velocity** | 50% gain | Exit day of expiration if TP not hit |
| **Velocity** | 50% gain | 50% loss |
| **Two-Way Trigger** | 20% gain (combined) | Close after 10 trading days if not hit |

- **Bull Surge** — Catches stocks when momentum's about to accelerate
- **Bear Plunge** — Catches stocks at the perfect moment before the floor drops
- **U-Turn** — Stocks consolidating after pullback, selling exhausts, buyers step in. Hold to expiration if needed.
- **U-Turn Velocity** — Faster/stronger version of U-Turn with momentum confirmation. Hold to expiration if needed.
- **Two-Way Trigger** — Volatility play (strangle/straddle) when direction unclear but movement certain. Time-based exit after 10 trading days.
- **Velocity** — Momentum acceleration pattern

## Strategy Types

### Single-Leg (Long Call, Long Put)
- One contract, one direction
- Sheet: strike in Leg 2 column, Type = "C" or "P"

### Spreads (Call Spread, Put Spread)
- Two strikes, directional with defined risk
- Sheet: Leg 1 = short strike (higher for calls, lower for puts), Leg 2 = long strike

### Strangles / Straddles (Two-Way Trigger)
- Strangle: OTM call + OTM put | Straddle: same strike for both
- Combined debit = total cost
- Often time-based exit rather than dollar stop

## BUY Alert Structure

1. **HEADLINE:**
   `TRADE ALERT: [TICKER] [Pattern] Just Fired — [X]% Pattern`

2. **OPENING (tension-first, 1-2 sentences):**
   Market is wrong + pattern caught it. Example:
   "Everyone's betting against [TICKER]. The [Pattern] says they're wrong."
   OR: "This [sector] stock just triggered our [X]% pattern — for the second time."

3. **Pattern intro (2-3 sentences):**
   - Current price + pattern fired
   - **Company context sentence** — one sentence that tells the reader what they need to know about the company AND the setup. This is NOT a formula — each trade is different:
     - **Mispricing angle** (BAC): "Bank of America is the second-largest bank in the U.S., and right now it's sitting on a classic post-earnings overreaction that's begging to snap back."
     - **Hidden catalyst** (MGM): "MGM runs the most iconic portfolio on the Las Vegas Strip, and the market is treating it like a dead trade while BetMGM quietly turned its first profit."
     - **Re-trigger** (TSLA): Can skip the company intro — subscribers know Tesla. Lead with the pattern re-firing at a better price.
     - **Obscure ticker** (HNRG): Needs more context — subscribers may not know the company.
   - **Match the sentence to the trade. Don't force a mispricing frame when it doesn't fit.**
   - This sentence is flexible — it can come early (after the price line) or be woven into the opener. The point is the reader has enough context to understand the opportunity.

4. **Pattern stats (1 paragraph):**
   - Win-loss over past X years, total returns %
   - Last time it fired on this stock + result
   - "And the [Pattern] just triggered right now." OR "It just triggered again."

5. **[CUSTOM BOLD HEADLINE — the story in one line]**
   3-4 short paragraphs. No bullets. One narrative thread.
   Specific facts, names, dollar amounts. First-person where it lands.
   Connect to WHY the pattern timing is right NOW.

6. **Tie-in before ACTION:**
   One sentence bridging fundamentals to the trade. Flexible — it can vary by alert, but the function is the same: connect the pattern to the specific fundamental reason it's firing NOW. Don't copy the same line every time.
   Examples: "When Bull Surge triggers with real fundamental acceleration — that's when it delivers." / "When U-Turn Velocity triggers with this much momentum behind it — that's when it delivers." / "The pattern that's never lost just fired again."

7. **ACTION:**
   ```
   ACTION: Buy-to-Open [TICKER] [EXPIRATION] $[STRIKE] [Call/Put] at $[ENTRY] or better
   Take Profit: $[TARGET] ([X]% gain)
   Stop Loss: $[STOP] ([X]% loss)
   ```
   Optional spread alternative after.

7. **CLOSING (2-3 sentences):**
   Days until expiration. Pattern conviction. "Get in, get the [X]%, get out."

8. **"What This Trade Looks Like"** (section header for screenshot — always include)

## SELL Alert Structure

**⚠️ QC REQUIREMENT:** Before writing any sell alert, read the two most recent OI sell alerts from WordPress. Match their structure exactly.

### Losers — Structure (bones are fixed, language is flexible)

**⚠️ Do NOT copy these openers verbatim. The structure is the guide — the language must fit THIS specific trade.**

```
1. OPENER: One punchy sentence — what the system expected vs. what actually happened.
   Direction: system called it wrong. Be specific to this trade, not generic.

2. CONTEXT (2-3 sentences):
   - What went wrong in the market/sector/macro since entry
   - Include specific price at trigger date, days elapsed, current price

3. STOP LINE: Confirm stop triggered. Short. "Time to act." or equivalent.

4. ACTION (bold): Sell-to-Close with specific price "at $X or better"

5. MATH: Entry → current, % down

6. RECORD: Pattern name, X-Y record, total returns %, "This is the Nth loss."

7. CLOSER:
   - ONE sentence on why this specific trade failed (sector selloff / macro reversal / wrong vehicle / etc.)
   - "The pattern works." is OPTIONAL — only use it if it actually fits. If the pattern itself failed, don't say it.
   - "Cut it. Clear the position. Next setup's already forming." — or your own variation

**⚠️ SELL alerts do NOT include "What This Trade Looks Like" — that's BUY alerts only (for the chart screenshot).**
**⚠️ SELL alerts do NOT include spacer blocks between paragraphs — uniform spacing throughout.**
```

**The structure is consistent. The voice is not. Each alert should sound like it was written for THIS trade, not assembled from a template.**
