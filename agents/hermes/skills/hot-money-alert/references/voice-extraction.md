Extracted Apr 3, 2026. Corpus: 118 paragraphs, 258 sentences, 2,329 words across 30 alerts.

## Sentence Rhythm (THE KEY FINDING)

**52% of all sentences are 8 words or fewer.** That's the dominant rhythm. Josh writes SHORT.

| Length | Count | % |
|--------|-------|---|
| 1-8 words | 135 | 52% |
| 9-18 words | 107 | 41% |
| 19+ words | 16 | **6%** |

**Only 6% of sentences exceed 18 words.** If your draft has more than 1 in 10 sentences over 18 words, it's too long. Cut or split.

Average words per sentence: **9.0**. Not 15. Not 20. Nine.

## Punchy Standalone Lines (The Voice's Signature)

95 standalone lines of ≤5 words across 30 alerts. These are the heartbeat Josh is always talking about. Examples:

**Flow/money:**
- "That money knew something."
- "That's not a hedge."
- "That's a thesis."
- "Following the flow."

**Stakes/urgency:**
- "No exit strategy."
- "Right now conditions aren't normal."
- "Three-day weekend ahead."
- "Same direction. Same day."

**Results:**
- "Oil just hit $111."
- "We take it."
- "That's how it's done."
- "Cut it."
- "Onto the next one."

**Rule: Every HMT alert should have 3-5 standalone punchy lines (≤5 words) sprinkled through the body.** They create the breathing room Josh keeps asking for.

## Paragraph Openers

Top opening words tell you the voice pattern:
- "And" (7x) — Josh starts paragraphs with "And." This is deliberate. It creates momentum between paragraphs.
- "Here's" (7x) — "Here's what caught my attention." "Here's the backdrop." "Here's what makes this activity stand out."
- "That's" (4x) — "That's a thesis." "That's the short-term money talking." "That's a signal worth following."
- "When" (4x) — Signal statements. "When this much money floods into one strike..."
- "But" (2x) — Pivot/contrast. "But here's what takes this from interesting to hard-to-ignore."

**Pattern: Short declarative openers, not subordinate clauses.** Not "While the market was selling off..." but "The market sold off." Direct.

## What I Was Getting Wrong

Based on this extraction vs. my drafts:

1. **My sentences were too long.** Josh averages 9 words/sentence. I was writing 15-20. Cut every sentence in half.
2. **Not enough standalone punchy lines.** Josh sprinkles 3-5 per alert. I was writing flowing prose without breathing room.
3. **Starting paragraphs with subordinate clauses.** Josh starts with "And", "That's", "But", "When" — direct kicks. Not "While the market was..."
4. **Dense paragraphs.** 48% of Josh's paragraphs are 15 words or fewer. My defaults are 40+ word paragraphs.
5. **"a single" as emphasis.** Josh uses "single strike," "single session," "single day" to hammer concentration. I wasn't doing this.
