# Daily Content — Formats & Examples by Day

---

## Monday — "What I'm Watching"

Real positions being considered. Why Josh might be wrong. Actual dollar amounts at risk.

**Template:**
> "SPY hit 600. I'm not buying. Here's the level where I would: $587. That's where the November seller dumped 50,000 contracts. If someone's defending that level with that much capital, I want to be there too."

**Rules:**
- Specific level, not "I'm bullish on SPY"
- Include why he might be wrong
- Real dollar amounts or contract sizes if available

---

## Tuesday — "The Trade I Didn't Take"

The near-miss. What almost happened and what stopped him. People learn from what Josh DOESN'T do.

**Template:**
> "Almost bought NVDA calls at open. The flow was there — $2M in weeklies. But the VIX wasn't confirming. Saved myself from a -30% day. Sometimes the best trades are the ones you skip."

**Rules:**
- What specifically he almost did
- What specifically stopped him (not vague)
- No "see, I'm so smart" energy — just the honest hesitation

---

## Wednesday — "Position Update"

Existing trades, real-time. Numbers, current status, what he's watching for.

**Template:**
> "Still holding my gold from $2,500. Down to $2,350 today. Not adding, not selling. Waiting for IV to drop below 20%. Currently at 27%. Patience sucks but it pays."

**Rules:**
- Real entry price, real current price
- What the decision is right now (holding, adding, thinking about closing)
- The honest internal debate if there is one

---

## Thursday — "Weird Shit I'm Seeing"

Unusual flow, strange data, something that caught his eye. NOT "here's what's REALLY happening" — just: "this is weird, here's what I think."

**Template:**
> "Someone's buying January 2026 calls on a uranium miner that hasn't produced anything in 3 years. $400K worth. Either they're insane or know something about nuclear policy. Watching, not touching."

**Rules:**
- "This is weird" energy, not "I have the inside scoop"
- No claiming special knowledge
- Sometimes the observation is the whole post — no conclusion needed

---

## Friday — "Week's Reality Check"

Honest accounting. Total P&L, biggest win, biggest loss, what's being held over the weekend. No spin.

**Template:**
> "Made $3,200 on boring SPY puts. Lost $8,100 trying to catch the Tesla knife. Net: -$4,900. The boring trades worked. The exciting ones didn't. There's a lesson there."

**Rules:**
- Net P&L first
- One win, one loss called out specifically
- What's held over the weekend (adds stakes)
- A bad week IS the content — never spin it

## Bonus Formats (Use Any Day When Applicable)

**Technical Setup With History**
> "This pattern burned me three times last year. Here's why I'm watching it again anyway..."
> "Saw this exact setup in February 2020 right before I completely missed the move. Watching it now."

**Market Observation With Memory**
> "Weird flow in tech today. Reminds me of February 2020 when I completely missed..."
> "Volume just died. Like someone unplugged the market. Last time I saw this was..."

**Lucky vs. Good**
> "Made $8K on NVDA yesterday. Pure luck — I was actually wrong about the direction but got saved by the broader move. Counting it. Not proud of it."

**The Specificity Principle (applies to all formats)**
The difference between dead content and engaging content is specificity:
- "Gold is going up" → useless
- "China's hoarding gold, central banks dumping dollars, and here's the EXACT February call option that could triple your money" → NOW we're talking

Always include: specific price levels, specific dates, specific dollar amounts, specific contract counts. Vague is boring. Specific is memorable.

---

## Universal Rules for Every Day

- One specific thing per post — never everything
- Real prices, real numbers — never vague
- Start with the most interesting fact
- Write like texting a friend who trades
- Show confusion/hesitation/mistakes — that's content
- Never position as knowing secrets
- Some days nothing happened. "Did nothing today. Watched everything move. Sometimes the best trade is no trade." — that works.
