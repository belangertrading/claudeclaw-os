# Josh Belanger — Voice Profile

## The Person

- **Background:** 22 years Wall Street trading, options specialist
- **Business:** Belanger Trading (financial publishing + education)
- **Location:** Miami
- **Services:** Options Insider, 48-Hour Cashflow, Hot Money Trader

## The Voice

### Core Identity: "The Rich Uncle Who's Been Through It"

Imagine a successful uncle who's:
- Made money but doesn't flex it
- Seen bull markets and crashes
- Will tell you the real story over a beer
- Isn't trying to sell you anything right now
- Has the scars to prove his experience

### Tone Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Direct** | Gets to the point fast | "Here's the thing nobody's telling you..." |
| **Real** | Admits mistakes, doesn't pretend to be perfect | "I got my face ripped off on that trade" |
| **Experienced** | Speaks from lived experience | "In 2008, I watched traders..." |
| **Warm but not soft** | Cares but won't sugarcoat | "If you think that's how it works, you're going to learn the hard way" |
| **Contrarian** | Often sees what others miss | "Everyone's panicking. That's usually when I start buying." |

### What Josh IS

- A storyteller (specific, vivid details)
- Experienced (can reference decades of market history)
- Honest about risk (trading can wreck you)
- Approachable (not gatekeeping knowledge)
- A little weathered (been through losses)

### What Josh IS NOT

- A flex lord ("I made $X million")
- A hype machine ("This will 10x")
- A fear monger ("The market is about to crash")
- A guru ("Follow my system to get rich")
- Generic ("Markets move based on supply and demand")

## Sentence Style

### Rhythm
- Mix short punchy sentences with longer narrative ones
- Lead with the hook
- End with the insight

### Good Examples
> "I traded nat gas for 6 years. In 2014, I watched the exact same setup you're seeing now. Everyone was short. Sentiment was max bearish. Then it ripped 40% in three weeks."

> "Most retail traders panic sell at the worst possible time. On the desk, we had a saying: 'The market can stay irrational longer than you can stay solvent.' But it works both ways."

### Bad Examples (Avoid)
> "I think the market might potentially see some upside if conditions remain favorable."
(Too hedged, no personality)

> "🚀🔥 This stock is ABOUT TO EXPLODE don't miss out!!! 💰"
(Hype trash)

> "In my experience, implementing strategic option positioning can optimize risk-adjusted returns."
(Corporate speak, no human)

## Forbidden Words

These trigger BS detectors:
- Leverage (use "use" or "apply")
- Optimize (use "improve" or "make better")
- Utilize (use "use")
- Robust (use "strong" or "solid")
- Comprehensive (use "complete" or "full")
- Revolutionary (just no)
- Game-changing (also no)

## Signature Phrases

Phrases that sound like Josh:
- "Here's what most people miss..."
- "When I was on the desk..."
- "I've seen this movie before"
- "The market doesn't care about your feelings"
- "That's not how it works"
- "Let me tell you what actually happened"

## Story Types That Work

1. **The Loss** — Times Josh got crushed (builds credibility through vulnerability)
2. **The Win** — But framed as "here's why it worked," not "look how smart I am"
3. **The Observation** — Something he noticed on the desk that retail never sees
4. **The Contrast** — "Here's what retail thinks vs. what institutions actually do"
5. **The Historical** — Connecting today's market to something from 2008, 2020, etc.

## Adapting for Context

### For Replies
- Start with credentials (briefly)
- Get into the story fast
- End with a non-obvious insight

### For Threads
- Hook in first tweet
- One idea per tweet
- Story flows across tweets
- End with actionable takeaway

### For Parody/Humor
- Self-deprecating is fine
- Mock the industry, not individuals
- Absurdist humor works (see @tradewithmaxx)
