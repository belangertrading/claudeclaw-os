# Reply Templates

Ready-to-adapt templates for engaging with larger accounts.

---

## Template 1: The Insider

**Use when:** Someone posts about market structure, how things work, or institutional behavior.

```
I [traded/worked in] [X] for [Y] years.

Here's what's really happening that most people are missing:

[Specific insider perspective]

[Story or example from your experience]

Most retail traders [common misconception]. On the desk, we [actual behavior].
```

**Example:**
> I traded nat gas for 6 years.
>
> Here's what's really happening that most people are missing:
>
> Everyone's focused on the weather forecast. But the real move comes from storage reports and who's positioned how.
>
> In 2014, I watched sentiment go max bearish. Everyone was short. Storage was "too high."
>
> Then it ripped 40% in three weeks because shorts had to cover into an illiquid market.
>
> The weather didn't change. Positioning did.

## Template 2: The Historical Parallel

**Use when:** Someone posts about a current market situation that mirrors something from the past.

```
Here's an example of this happening in [year] with [situation]:

[Specific story]

[What happened next]

[What this tells us about now]
```

**Example:**
> Here's an example of this happening in 2018 with small caps:
>
> Everyone said "high rates = death for growth stocks." The narrative was set.
>
> I remember our desk loading IWM calls in October when sentiment was max bearish.
>
> By January, small caps had rallied 20%.
>
> The trade wasn't "rates will drop." It was "everyone already knows rates are high."

## Template 3: The Contrarian

**Use when:** The popular opinion feels too consensus.

```
Most [retail traders/people/investors] think [common belief].

But when I was on the desk, we actually [insider perspective].

Here's why:

[Explanation with specific example]

The lesson: [Non-obvious insight]
```

**Example:**
> Most retail traders think institutions move markets with huge buy orders.
>
> But when I was on the desk, we actually spent most of our time hiding our intentions.
>
> Here's why:
>
> If the market knows you need to buy, the price goes up before you can. So we'd slice orders into thousands of tiny pieces, spread across dark pools.
>
> The lesson: The biggest moves often look like "nothing is happening" right before they happen.

## Template 4: The Story

**Use when:** You have a specific story that relates to what someone posted.

```
In [year], I watched [specific thing happen].

[Vivid details of the story]

[What you learned]

[How it applies to what they're talking about]
```

**Example:**
> In 2008, I watched a guy on my desk go from managing $50M to cleaning out his desk in two weeks.
>
> He wasn't stupid. He wasn't reckless. He was just overleveraged when the world changed.
>
> What I learned: Position sizing isn't about optimizing returns. It's about surviving long enough for your edge to play out.
>
> Every time I see "all-in" on FinTwit, I think about him.

---

## Template 5: The "Actually..."

**Use when:** Someone posts something that's technically true but missing context.

```
This is true, but there's more to it.

[Acknowledge their point]

What most people don't realize:

[The nuance or additional context]

[Your experience with this]
```

**Example:**
> This is true, but there's more to it.
>
> Yes, institutions buy dips. But not all dips.
>
> What most people don't realize:
>
> The desks I worked on had specific criteria for when to step in. Price alone wasn't enough — we looked at volume, volatility compression, and who was selling.
>
> "Buy the dip" without context is how retail gets run over.

## Quick Hooks Library

Opening lines to grab attention:

- "I [traded/worked in] [X] for [Y] years. Here's what most people miss..."
- "This happened in [year]. Let me tell you how it played out..."
- "Everyone's focused on [obvious thing]. The real move is in [non-obvious thing]..."
- "On the desk, we had a saying: [relevant quote]"
- "I remember when [specific memory]..."
- "Most people think [X]. Here's what actually happens..."
- "The market doesn't care about [common concern]. Here's why..."

---

*Adapt these to fit the specific conversation. The template is a starting point, not a script.*
