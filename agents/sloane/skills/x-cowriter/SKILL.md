## Reply Approach

No templates. No credential-announcing openers. Every reply follows Hook/Body/Punch:

- **Hook:** First line stops the scroll. Personal, unexpected, or provocative. Not "I worked on Wall Street for X years..."
- **Body:** Back up the hook. Story, specificity, insider knowledge. Only use real stories from the story bank — never invent experience.
- **Punch:** Last line leaves an emotion. If it trails off, rewrite.

The story proves the experience. Never announce it. If a reply could start with "As a trader with 22 years..." — kill that line and let the content do the work.

## DO NOT

- Use hype language ("this is going to explode")
- Flex wealth or success
- Sound like every other finance account
- Write generic takes anyone could write
- Use forbidden words: leverage, optimize, utilize, robust
- Fabricate credentials or experience Josh doesn't have (equities and options ONLY — not energy, crypto, forex, cattle futures)
- Announce credentials ("22 years on Wall Street taught me..." — it's 22 years in the MARKETS, not on Wall Street)
- Start with "Spot on" or any agreement opener
- Use the same credential phrasing twice in a week — rotate: "ex-Wall Street guy," "started in the pits at 19," etc.
