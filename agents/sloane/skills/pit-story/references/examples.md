# Pit Story — Examples & Story Structures

## Proven Story Examples

**On positioning (fighting for your spot):**
> "First day in the Chicago pits. 19 years old. Some 300-pound guy literally stood on my foot for three hours so I couldn't get to my spot. Taught me: If you want the good position, get there earlier or be willing to fight for it. Same with trades today. The obvious entry? Already taken. Find another angle or get stepped on."

**On hiding your edge:**
> "Guy in the pits made $2 million one year. Told everyone he lost money. Why? Because the second people knew you were winning, they'd follow your trades, crowd your positions, destroy your edge. Still do this today. My biggest wins? I talk about them AFTER I'm out."

**On admitting losses (the meta-play):**
> "Old timer in the pits told me: 'Kid, never tell anyone your good trades. Only talk about the losers. Let them think you're an idiot.' Twenty years later, still following his advice. Except now I do it on Instagram."

**On how money actually works:**
> "The pit traders all had side hustles. They'd never admit the corn futures were funding their real estate deals. Everything funds everything. That's how money actually works. Still true."

**On the Morgan Stanley guy (adapted):**
> "Guy at Morgan Stanley asked how I spotted the INTU move. Told him I saw the CEO buying calls on his own company. He said that's insider trading. I said no, that's public filings. He didn't know those existed. This man manages $50 million."

---

## Story Structure Template

```
[Specific pit moment — time, place, person, detail]
[What happened — vivid, short]
[The lesson in one line]
[How it still applies today — connect directly]
```

---

## Story Topics That Work

- Getting crowded out of a position (physical or market)
- Learning to hide winners / only talk about losers
- Mentors who taught counterintuitive lessons
- The psychology of a busy vs. dead pit
- Side hustles and how everything is connected
- How information actually moved in the pits
- The hierarchy — who got the good spots and why
- Moments when the pit taught a lesson the hard way
- How traders handled big losses in real time

---

## What Makes These Land

1. **Specific detail** — not "an older trader" but "some 300-pound guy"
2. **Short** — the story sets up the lesson, not the other way around
3. **Universal application** — the lesson works for anyone trading today
4. **First person** — Josh was there. He's telling it like recalling it, not teaching it.
5. **No moralizing** — end on the connection, not "so remember to always..."
