# Contrast Post — Examples & Rules

## Proven Examples

**The Frank example (the gold standard):**
> "My Subscribers Made 1,560%. I Lost $47,000. Market commentary through confession — I'll let you figure out who had the better week."

> "Frank donated $50 to my charity marathon. Got my gold report. Made 1,100%. I sold my gold position early. Left $30K on the table. Frank's up. I'm not. Anyway."

> "Lost another $15K today. Frank from the charity thing is up 1,100% on my gold call. I'm starting to think Frank should be teaching the course."

**The hedge fund version:**
> "Hedge fund manager DM'd asking for my gold thesis. Sent him the same email I sent Frank for donating $50 to charity. He paid $5,000 for the same charity report. Markets are perfect, they said."

**The returning subscriber:**
> "Had a subscriber cancel to follow some kid promising 1000% returns. He's back. Lost everything in three weeks. Now we're working on risk management. Welcome home."

**The service vs. personal trade:**
> "Options Insider subscribers are up on three of four open trades this week. My personal account is down $6K. I give better advice than I take. Story of my life."

**The dry Friday version:**
> "Worst trading week in a month. Subscribers had a good week. I'm starting to think they should be managing my money."

---

## When to Use This Format

- Subscriber result dramatically outperforms Josh's personal position on same trade
- Service alert hit while Josh personally missed or fumbled
- End of a bad week when subscribers held up better
- When the irony is genuinely funny and real
- Frank-type stories when a member outcome comes in

---

## What NEVER to Do

❌ "My subscribers made 1,560%! Join my service now for $99/month!"
❌ Manufacturing the contrast — if Josh didn't actually lose, don't pretend he did
❌ Claiming specific subscriber returns without documentation
❌ Making it an obvious pitch — it must feel like just telling the truth

The moment Josh tries to sell with this format, it dies. It works because it's genuinely what happened, told straight.

---

## The Psychology Behind It

Winners posting wins = annoying
Losers posting losses = pathetic  
Winners posting losses = fascinating

Everyone wonders: "How much does this guy actually have if he laughs about losing $30K?"
Josh never answers that. Just loses more money publicly.

The contrast post is the ultimate version of this — he's clearly doing something right for his subscribers, and clearly doing something dumb with his own money. Both true. Both real. Both compelling.
