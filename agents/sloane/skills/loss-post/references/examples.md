# Loss Post — Types, Hooks & Examples

## Loss Types (Use the Right One)

**Bad entry**
> "Bought the literal top. Gold at $2,650. Two hours later it's at $2,580. Classic."

**Bad exit**
> "Sold NVDA at $700. It's at $950. I gave myself a reason to sell and the market gave me a reason to regret it."

**Missed trade**
> "Didn't take my own alert on Tuesday. It's up 40%. There's no good explanation for this."

**Death by a thousand cuts**
> "Five small losses this week. Each one felt manageable. Together they're one big loss. That math always surprises me."

**Opportunity cost**
> "Sat in cash all week while the market ripped. Didn't lose a dollar. Still feels like I lost."

**Too early exit**
> "Up 50%, sold, now it's up 400%. Disciplined exit. Terrible outcome. Both things are true."

## The Realistic Weekly Rhythm

Not every loss is a $47K blowup. Vary it:

Monday: "Down $3K at open. Cool cool cool."
Tuesday: "Tesla chopping around. Still underwater."
Wednesday: "Gold calls keeping me alive while Tesla bleeds."
Thursday: "Closed Tesla. -$8K. Moving on."
Friday: "Frank texted. He's up another 20% on gold. I sold mine Tuesday."

## The Real Trader Contradictions (High-Engagement Content)

The cognitive dissonance of actual trading. These posts hit because everyone feels them but nobody says them out loud:

- "You hate a company but love the stock"
- "You know you should sell but can't"
- "You see a perfect setup but you're gun-shy from your last loss"
- "You're making money on a trade you don't understand"
- "Your worst trade is working. Your best research is failing."
- "Volume just died. Like someone unplugged the market."
- "Every Chinese stock is up 5% except the one I own."

Write about the actual experience of trading — the confusion, the near-misses, the contradictions. Not pretending to have special insight every day.

---

## Dumbest Trade of the Week (Deep Dive Format)

Full breakdown of the worst decision of the week. Mental errors, what happened, what should've happened.

**Why it works:** Makes losing subscribers feel better about their own losses. Makes winning subscribers realize everyone takes hits.

**Structure:**
1. The trade — what it was, entry, how bad it got
2. The mental error — specifically what went wrong in the thinking
3. Where it stands now (still holding, cut it, watching)
4. One honest line at the end — not a lesson, just the truth

> "Dumbest trade this week was [trade]. The mental error: [specific]. Still [status]. [One dry honest line]."

## The Legal Line

No returns claims. No percentages unless real and documented.

✅ "Lost $30K on Tesla this week. Still trading. Still here."
✅ "Down $30K on Tesla. The gold calls from last year are keeping me alive."
❌ "Down $30K but my subscribers made millions! Come join!"

The fact that Josh can lose $30K and post about it casually IS the credibility. No broke person does that. Let people do the math themselves.

---

## What Makes It Real vs. Parody

✅ "Lost $30K on Tesla" — real, hurts, still fine
✅ "Wife's getting concerned" — relatable
✅ "Down bad this month" — honest
❌ "Had to sell the Lambo" — meme territory
❌ "Wife's boyfriend said I need to stop" — WSB parody
❌ "Behind Wendy's dumpster" — internet joke

Real = connection that sticks. Parody = one-time laugh, then they leave.
