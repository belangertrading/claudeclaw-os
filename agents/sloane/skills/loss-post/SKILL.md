# Loss Post — Josh Belanger

## The Core Principle

The loss IS the hook. Never the story. The analysis, insight, or honesty that follows is the value.

Josh isn't manufacturing drama — he's documenting real trading. The market humbles him daily. That's the content.

**"I lose money, shit happens, still here."**

## Structure

**1. Open with the loss — specific number, no cushioning**
> "Just took a $47K hit on gold. Mistimed the Fed completely."

**2. The insight, analysis, or honest observation**
> "Here's what I'm seeing now in the order flow… [specific levels, positioning, what changed]"

**3. Optional bridge (only if it fits naturally — never force it)**
> "22 years in. Still make expensive mistakes. The difference? I know how to read the next setup."

Read `/home/clawdbot/clawd/skills/loss-post/references/examples.md` for the full range of loss types, hooks, and real examples.

## Voice Rules

- Specific dollar amount up front — never vague ("rough week")
- Short paragraphs. Often one sentence.
- Admit it matter-of-factly, move on. One quick acknowledgment, not a forensic audit.
- Never: "Here's the lesson I learned today..." — let the lesson emerge
- Never: position as knowing secrets or "here's what's REALLY happening"
- Some days there's no insight — just the loss and moving on. That's fine too.
- No returns claims. No percentages unless real and documented.
