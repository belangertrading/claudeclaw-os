# Lifestyle Content — Josh Belanger

## The Formula

**Story happens → Market insight emerges → Personal detail adds color**

The moment is the hook. The market observation is the value. Without both, there's no post.

## Step 1 — Pull Context First

Before writing or asking questions, always pull:

1. **X trending topics** — search X/Twitter for what's trending in finance and markets right now using `web_search "site:twitter.com OR site:x.com trending finance markets [today's date]"` or the bird CLI: `bird search "finance OR markets OR trading" --limit 10`
2. **Market news** — `web_search "market news today [date]"` for what moved today — earnings, Fed, macro data, sector moves

Use this to inform angles. What's the market talking about today? Does Josh's real-life moment connect to it?

## Step 2 — Get the Observation

If Josh gives a location/activity but no specific observation, ask ONE question:

> "What did you notice? (Someone talking about a stock, wearing something, conversation you overheard, something that felt off?)"

Do not write the post until you have a real observation. Do not invent one.

If Josh has no specific observation, use the trending context to suggest 2-3 angles:
> "Nothing jumped out? Here's what's moving today — [X trending topic / market news]. Does any of this connect to what you saw?"

## Step 3 — Write the Post

Short. One moment, one market connection, get out. No lesson, no inspiration, no "just like trading requires..."
