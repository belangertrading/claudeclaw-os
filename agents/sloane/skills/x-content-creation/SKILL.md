## CONTENT TYPES

### 1. Standard Tweet (Under 280 chars)
**When:** Quick market takes, one-liner observations, reactions to news.

**Structure:** Hook → Story → Punch
- **Hook:** First line must stop the scroll. Contrarian, specific, or pattern-interrupt.
- **Story:** 1-3 lines of context, setup, or evidence.
- **Punch:** Final line delivers the emotional hit. Makes them engage.

**Rules:**
- Every tweet must pass the "would I stop scrolling for this?" test
- No throwaway content. Every post = expected value when they see the PFP.
- Vary line lengths. Short line. Then a slightly longer one that builds. Then short again.
- Line breaks between thoughts. Not after every sentence — between thought shifts.

**Examples of good hooks:**
- "I got fired from my first job." (personal, vulnerable, makes you want more)
- "The crypto space is absolutely toxic." (polarizing, makes you pick a side)
- "If you're not carving out 20 minutes a day to reply to big accounts, you're playing X on hard mode." (challenges the reader directly)

**Examples of BAD hooks:**
- "You will be forgotten." (generic motivational, every personal brand account posts this)
- "In 100 years, you're going to be dead." (ChatGPT personal branding speak)
- Anything that could come from any account. If you remove @BelangerTrading and can't tell who wrote it, kill it.

### 2. Long-Form Tweet (Over 280 chars)
**When:** Personal stories, trade breakdowns, motivational with substance, suspense-driven takes.

**Structure:**
1. **Character introduction** (usually Josh)
2. **Goal** (what he was trying to do)
3. **Challenge** (what stopped him)
4. **Plan** (how he approached breaking through)
5. **Conflict** (what happened when he tried)
6. **Transformation/Punch** (the result or lesson)

**Critical: Show More Button Placement**
The Show More button appears at the 280-char mark. This is the MOST important element of a long-form tweet. 90% of performance depends on where this button falls.

**How to control placement:**
- Write your hook (what appears above Show More)
- Find the line break where you want the button
- Add spaces after that line to push to exactly 280 characters
- Then write the rest below

**Ideal Show More placements:**
- Right after "Here's the secret." or "Let me explain." or "Here's what happened next."
- After a cliffhanger: "Best thing that ever happened to me." [Show More]
- After raising stakes: "Imagine this scenario." [Show More]

**Rules:**
- Long-form doesn't mean novel. 281 characters with a compelling Show More beats 1000 characters of rambling.
- Use the same line break / varying length principles as standard tweets
- Personal stories perform best in this format
- End with a punch that makes them engage

### 3. Thread
**When:** Step-by-step guides, tutorials, listicles, multimedia-heavy content, resource lists.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook tweet** — First line of first tweet = most important line of entire thread. Challenge → Agitate → Promise to Solve.
2. **Introduction tweet** — Table of contents / foreshadow what's covered. Optional early CTA (bookmark this).
3. **Body tweets** — Each chapter gets a visual cue emoji (pick one, stick with it). Story section → Action items per chapter.
4. **Punch tweet** — Emotional takeaway, transformation, final statement.
5. **CTA tweet(s)** — Newsletter subscribe, retweet request. You EARNED this by giving value.

**Best practices:**
- 12-15 tweets is the sweet spot. Under 25 max.
- Use visual cue emojis to mark chapter breaks (🔸 or similar — pick one and own it)
- Multimedia in every tweet if possible — algorithm boosts images/video
- Vary structure within tweets: bullets, lists, arrows, bold questions
- Don't ask for bookmarks on every thread — only when there's genuine reason to revisit
- Live threads (adding to it over time) create urgency and return visits
- Newsletter plug can go mid-thread OR at end — test both

**Thread types that work:**
- Step-by-step tutorials (how to do X)
- Listicles (10 things you need to know about X)
- Resource lists (7 free tools for X) — but DON'T link out, just name them
- Story with chapters + multimedia
- Conversational (ask yourself questions the reader would ask, then answer)

## CONTENT CREATION PROCESS

### Step 1: Ideation
Sources for ideas (in order of priority):
1. **Josh's raw input** — brain dumps, voice notes, trade ideas, observations
2. **Tweet database** — review past @BelangerTrading tweets, see what performed, remix
3. **Inspiration accounts** — study how similar accounts write, steal structures not content
4. **Swipe file** — saved tweets with interesting hooks/structures to emulate
5. **Brain dump** — 30 min free-write on a topic, then extract tweet ideas from it

### Step 2: Draft
- Write the hook FIRST. Spend most time here.
- For long-form: place the Show More button before writing the rest
- For threads: write hook tweet, then outline chapters, then fill in
- Apply voice DNA throughout — read it out loud, does it sound like Josh?

### Step 3: Review (QC Checklist)
Before any content goes to Josh:

- [ ] **Personality test:** Cover the @BelangerTrading name. Can you tell it's Josh? If not, rewrite.
- [ ] **Boring test:** Would a generic personal brand account post this? If yes, kill it or add personality.
- [ ] **Focus test:** Does it go in ONE direction? Multiple directions = death. (See "bad tweets" analysis — unfocused content tanks.)
- [ ] **Hook test:** Would someone stop scrolling? First line must arrest attention.
- [ ] **Punch test:** Does the last line make them want to engage? Emotional hit required.
- [ ] **Line break test:** Are thoughts separated? Are line lengths varied? No text blocks.
- [ ] **Specificity test:** Real tickers? Real numbers? Real stories? Generic = dead.
- [ ] **Show More test (long-form only):** Is the button placed at a cliffhanger?
- [ ] **No outside links in tweet body** — add in reply 10-30 min later if needed
- [ ] **No ChatGPT speak** — no "let me be clear," no "here's the thing," no motivational platitudes

## METRICS HIERARCHY (What to Optimize For)

Leading indicators (you control these):
1. **Time on content** — longest possible read time. Long-form + threads + no outside links.
2. **Bookmarks** — actionable content people want to revisit. Tips, guides, frameworks.
3. **Replies** — polarizing takes, questions, conversational content.

Trailing indicators (outcomes of the above):
4. Retweets
5. Likes  
6. Impressions

**Rule:** Optimize for #1-3. The rest follows.

## POSTING STRATEGY

### Daily Cadence
- 2 quality tweets per day (not more — every post should = expected value)
- 4 long-form tweets per week (sprinkled across the 2 daily)
- 2 threads per week
- Reply volume scales with platform priority — X is one channel in a multi-platform strategy. Quality over quantity, always. If Josh has 15 min for X, that's 5-10 great replies, not 100 mid ones.
- 1 DM per day to someone you admire (optional — only when it's genuine)

### Timing
- Most activity: 9 AM - 12 PM ET weekdays
- BUT quality > timing. Best content posted at 9 PM Friday has gone viral.
- Retweet yourself ~9 hours after posting (algorithm refresh)
- Reply to your own tweet 3-4 hours later with additional color (resurfaces content)
- Outside links: wait 10-30 min, add in reply (never in original tweet)

### Scheduling
- Carve out 30 min/day for ideating + scheduling
- Schedule ahead so you're not thinking about content all day
- Use scheduling for vacations / busy periods

## REPLY STRATEGY ($2 Strategy)

### Setup
Two lists:
1. **Hunting list** — accounts you want to build relationships with (similar niche, similar or larger size)
2. **Farming list** — accounts that already engage with you regularly

### Rules
- Reply volume matches platform priority. X is one channel — don't over-index. A few great replies beat dozens of forgettable ones.
- NEVER just emoji or "great point!" — must be substantive, show your perspective
- Space replies out (20-30 seconds between). Rapid-fire = shadow ban risk.
- Don't reply to accounts below the "Show more replies" fold (shadow banned accounts hurt your reputation)
- Check follower/following ratio before engaging (bots/low-rep accounts drag your score down)
- Reply content should give a taste of YOUR content — if they hover your name, they should want to follow

## CONTENT KILLERS (What NOT to Do)

1. **Unfocused tweets** — going in 3 directions in one post. Pick ONE point.
2. **Generic motivational** — "Stop wasting time doing shit that doesn't bring you happiness." Every account posts this.
3. **No personality** — if you remove the name and PFP, no one knows it's Josh. Dead content.
4. **Humor that kills the point** — personality should enhance the message, not replace it.
5. **Reusing hooks that worked last year** — the meta changes. What went viral 12 months ago may be saturated now.
6. **Text blocks** — no line breaks = no one reads it.
7. **Outside links in the tweet** — algorithm deprioritizes. Always put in reply.
