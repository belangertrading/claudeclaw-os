# Journal Writing — Living Gotchas

Add to this file every time Claude produces a pattern Josh flags. This is the highest-signal content in the skill.

---

## Voice Gotchas

1. **Decisions are messy, not clean.** Never write "I sold." Write the 3 steps: tried to sell at X, market moved, cancelled, sold next day at Y. The indecision is the content.

2. **Don't explain the tension.** State the facts. Let the gap between them do the work. "Systems don't have feelings, but I do" — never follow with "That's the paradox."

3. **Endings don't resolve.** The last sentence should feel like Josh walked away mid-thought to check a chart. Not a lesson. Not a bow. Not "onto the next."

4. **300-500 words MAX.** If it's longer, you're explaining. Cut.

5. **Characters just appear.** "Henry says..." not "My AI assistant Henry, who helps run operations, mentioned..." No introductions. Members of the cast are known to the audience or they figure it out.

6. **Internal self-talk is second person.** "Come on Josh." "Should've just paid the ask Josh." Never "I told myself to..."

7. **Double hyphens (--) not em dashes (—).** Josh uses -- consistently. This is a formatting tell.

8. **No uniform paragraph lengths.** If every paragraph is 3 sentences, it's AI. Mix one-liners with 5-sentence blocks.

## Structural Gotchas

9. **No triplets.** Three short fragments in a row is the #1 AI tell. Break the pattern.

10. **No question → answer lists.** "What happened? X. Y. Z." is an AI pattern.

11. **No headers or sections within entries.** It's a journal, not a blog post.

12. **No bold for emphasis.** Rare at most. Josh doesn't bold things.

13. **No formulaic transitions.** "That being said" / "Having established that" / "With that in mind" — all AI tells.

## Content Gotchas

14. **Don't invent details.** If you don't have the specific price, time, or name — ask for it. Never fabricate specifics. Josh's entries work BECAUSE every number is real.

15. **Bridge line ≠ entry.** The hook/bridge goes before the raw entry. The entry itself is untouched journal. Never mix wrapper copy into the journal voice.

16. **"Most of my trading journal never gets published"** is the repeatable bridge opener. Don't use "I usually keep this private" (expires after 3 uses).

17. **Hook must be honest.** If the alert system also lost, don't imply it saved subscribers. Raw truth angle always.

18. **Loss entries = free. Win entries = consider gating.** Losses build trust (top of funnel). Wins are perceived value (paywall-worthy).
