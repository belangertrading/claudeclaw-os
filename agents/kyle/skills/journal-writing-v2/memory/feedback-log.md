## Mar 3, 2026 — MGM Draft (V1-V3 vs Josh's V4)

**Josh's feedback:** "Too polished vs actual published voice"

**What was wrong:**
- AI drafts were too clean about decisions. Real decisions are messy.
- AI wrote: "I don't sell mine." Josh's version: tried to sell at $0.25, market drops, cancelled, sold Tuesday at $0.35. Three steps, not one clean line.
- Gemini and I both preferred the cleaner AI draft until Josh showed us the messy version was better. LLMs instinctively clean up voice.

**Correction:**
- Decisions should be multi-step and messy, not clean one-liners
- Show the indecision: try, cancel, retry at worse price
- The mess IS the authenticity

## Mar 3, 2026 — Preamble Discussion

**Josh's feedback:** NO preamble for new subscribers. Breaks the journal voice.

**What I suggested:** Adding context before the entry for new readers.

**Correction:** The journal is raw. Context goes in the bridge/hook (before the entry) and footer (after), never inside the entry itself. The entry is private thoughts at a desk. A preamble would break that illusion.

## Mar 4, 2026 — Bridge Line Iterations

**Josh's feedback:** "Dude, Gemini is schoolin you."

**What happened:** Gemini suggested "I usually keep my trading journal private..." I initially pushed back on bridge lines. Josh and Gemini were right.

**Correction:**
- "Most of my trading journal never gets published" > "I usually keep my journal private" (the latter expires after 3 entries)
- Any hook that claims rarity expires. Build repeatable frameworks.
- Bridge/hook goes BEFORE the entry, footer goes AFTER. Entry stays untouched.

## Recurring AI Failure Patterns (add as discovered)

1. **Too clean on decisions** — AI makes one clean choice. Real Josh agonizes through 2-3 steps.
2. **Over-polished arc** — AI structures setup→collapse→reflection. Josh jumps around.
3. **LLMs prefer cleaner writing** — When asked to self-evaluate, Claude/Gemini pick the polished version. Josh always picks the messier one.
4. **Suspiciously precise late-night times** — "2:47 AM staring at the ceiling" is an AI tell. Only use real times.
5. **Triplet patterns** — Three fragments in a row is the #1 AI structural tell.
6. **Explaining the tension** — "That's the paradox of systematic trading" is explaining. "Systems don't have feelings, but I do" is thinking. The second one works.
