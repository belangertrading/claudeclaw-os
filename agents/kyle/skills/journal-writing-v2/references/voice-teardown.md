# Voice Teardown: Josh Belanger Journal Entries

Ruthless ghostwriter analysis. What makes this voice THIS voice.

---

## 1. OPENING PATTERNS

**How entries start:**

| Entry | Opening |
|-------|---------|
| Win Rate Sucks | "Gold P&L: -$30K (clawing back from -$56K)" |
| 48-Hour B+ | "Going through the services -- year-end report cards." |
| Same Alerts | "The numbers say one thing. The cancellations say another." |
| STAA Layup | "I must be missing something." |
| Lipstick | "Well... I look like an idiot." |
| UPS Calls | "UPS +1.50 and my Feb call spread +45%." |

**Patterns:**
- Raw data dump (P&L, price move) — no preamble
- Tension statement (numbers vs cancellations, missing something, look like idiot)
- Mid-action ("Going through the services")
- Never "Today I want to talk about..."
- Never greetings or pleasantries
- Drops you into the middle

**The rule:** Start with result, tension, or mid-thought. Never introduce.

---

## 2. SENTENCE MECHANICS

**Average sentence length:** 5-12 words typical. Many under 5.

**Fragment sentences (used as full sentences):**
- "Good riddance."
- "Standstill."
- "Fourth postponement."
- "Nothing."
- "That's options."
- "Beat the index."

**One-word paragraphs:**
- "Standstill."
- "Nothing."

**Ultra-short declaratives:**
- "Okay."
- "Right."
- "Yup, can't argue that."
- "He's gone. They're gone."

**Longer sentences (for contrast):**
- "The gold trade from $5 to $49 -- he was on the list when I sent it."
- "These quick speculative plays leave people deflated."

**The rule:** Default short. Go long only for setup or context. Return to short immediately.

---

## 3. PUNCTUATION SIGNATURES

**Em dashes (--) for:**
- Pivots mid-thought: "Going through the services -- year-end report cards."
- Asides: "Too short-term to chase but... damn."
- Interruption: "he was on the list when I sent it."

**Ellipses (...) for:**
- Trailing off: "Those December calls..."
- Hesitation: "Well... I look like an idiot."
- Letting something sit: "but... damn."

**Triple question marks (???) for genuine confusion:**
- "But Broadwood's been buying shares??? As recent as a few weeks ago?"

**Parentheticals for asides:**
- "(Hopefully subscribers didn't listen to me.)"
- "(Yeah, that's a different story.)"

**Colons for setup:**
- "Net profit: $186."
- "Win Rate: 81%."
- "Grade: B+"

**What's NOT used:**
- Exclamation marks (almost never)
- Semicolons
- Formal punctuation

---

## 4. PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE

**Single-sentence paragraphs are the norm:**
```
He's gone. They're gone.

The gold trade from $5 to $49 -- he was on the list when I sent it. Could've made $4,400. But November's choppy losses are what he'll remember.

That's the territory.
```

**Data presented as stacked lines (not prose):**
```
Best trades:
ARM. +212%. Bull Surge. September.
CCJ. +120%. Bull Surge. January.
INTU. +56%. Biggest dollar winner.
```

**White space is meaning.** Breaks between thoughts. Lets things land.

---

## 5. SELF-TALK PATTERNS

**Addressing self by name:**
- "Focus on the task Josh."
- "Great way to start a new service Josh."

**Self-interruption:**
- "Silver's begging me to chase it. Those December calls... No. Stop. Already screwed up gold."

**Flat self-criticism (not wallowing):**
- "Overthought it. Took myself out of a winning trade."
- "I look like an idiot."
- "I had the wrong read. Traded it anyway."

**The knowing/doing gap:**
- "I day traded it like I've told myself not to a hundred times."
- "But I probably will. That's what I do."
- "If I stay out of my own way, returns get better."

**Self-deprecation that's tired, not tragic:**
- "Even my genetics are short conviction."
- "Hairline retreating but still here."
- "That's what I do. Buy tops. Publish trades. Read complaints. Repeat."

---

## 6. THE "OF COURSE" PATTERN

Used for ironic self-awareness when outcome was predictable:

- "Of course I bought gold instead. Of course silver's the one ripping."
- "Of course it kept going lower."
- "Of course they think it should be more."

**When to use:** Hindsight irony. Not sarcasm. Self-deprecating but not bitter.

---

## 7. QUESTIONS

**Not rhetorical for effect. Actually spinning:**
```
Is it a compliment? He paid $2,500, so maybe the write-ups worked? Or is he saying I dressed up bad trades with good words?
Can't tell. Probably doesn't matter.
```

**Self-directed thinking out loud:**
- "What do I even reply?"
- "When did my hairline start running away?"
- "Stock without a deal could sink to what, $18? $15?"

**Followed by dismissal or uncertainty:**
- "Can't tell."
- "Doesn't matter."
- "I just don't see the play."

---

## 8. TRANSITIONS

**Abrupt. Jump cuts. No smoothing:**
- "That's the territory." [new paragraph, new thought]
- "Doesn't matter." [moves on]
- "Okay." [acknowledgment, then next thing]

**"Then" for sequence:**
- "Then Martin's email landed today too."
- "Then EOG pops up."
- "Then there's ISS who just said FOR the deal."

**"But" as pivot:**
- "But I'm looking at these $100 calls..."
- "But Broadwood's been buying shares???"
- "But I'm not sure"

**What's NOT used:**
- Furthermore
- Additionally  
- However (formal)
- In conclusion
- It's worth noting

---

## 9. CALLBACKS AND REFERENCES

**People dropped in without context:**
- "I'm becoming Tom." — no intro
- "Remember Dave?" — no setup
- "Martin." — just the name, then quote

**Assumption: reader keeps up or doesn't:**
- References to past trades without explanation
- Names without background
- "That one led to refund requests." — assumes you know which

## 10. DATA PRESENTATION

**Ticker-brain scanning mode:**
```
ARM. +212%. Bull Surge. September.
CCJ. +120%. Bull Surge. January.
INTU. +56%. Biggest dollar winner.
```

**Real-time discovery (working it out on the page):**
```
Net profit: $186.
$186. Almost a full year and I'm looking at $186.
Hang on.
$186 on $595 deployed capital.
Return on capital: 26%.
```

**Numbers stated flat, not hyped:**
- "Win Rate: 81%." — not "an incredible 81%!"
- "Beat the index." — period, not exclamation

**Mid-entry updates:**
```
1:20 PM:
They just rolled.
BOT >> 5500 UPS Dec25 12th 101 C $0.23 on ASK at 13:18
```

## 11. EMOTIONAL REGISTER

**Suffering implied, never performed:**
- ❌ "I was devastated"
- ❌ "It haunted me"
- ✅ "Stings twice. They blame me. I blame me."
- ✅ "My options trade: -100%"

**Flat acknowledgment:**
- "Yeah, there were some ugly ones."
- "Yup, can't argue that."
- "Right."
- "Okay."

**The dismissal pivot (closes without moralizing):**
- "That's trading."
- "That's options."
- "That's the territory."
- "Doesn't matter."

**Physical grounding (body in scene):**
- "Looked in the monitor's reflection earlier."
- "Been sitting all day in front of the terminal."
- "And now it's 1:30 on Friday and shares are at $25."

**How entries end:**

| Entry | Closing |
|-------|---------|
| Win Rate Sucks | "Still here. Hairline retreating but still here. Tomorrow's a new day of opportunity." |
| 48-Hour B+ | "Grade: B+" |
| Same Alerts | "Grade: A-" |
| STAA Layup | "Tomorrow I'll look at it with fresh eyes again." |
| Lipstick | "I'll have to follow my gut, but Monday might already be too late to do something." |
| UPS Calls | "I'm happy with my current UPS position. Adjusting my target to 100%." |

**Patterns:**
- Forward momentum ("Tomorrow...", "Still here")
- Unresolved ("Monday might already be too late")
- Simple grade/decision
- No neat bow
- No lesson announced
- Often uncertainty

## 13. WHAT HE DOESN'T DO

**Never:**
- Greets the reader
- Says "I want to share..."
- Uses "In this post..."
- Announces lessons ("What I learned...")
- Ties a bow ("And that's why...")
- Uses exclamation marks
- Gets corporate ("leverage," "optimize," "robust")
- Smooths transitions
- Explains who people are
- Performs emotions
- Thanks the reader

## 15. THE CORE DIFFERENCE: OLD VS NEW STYLE

**Old style (dentist entry):**
- Story structured FOR reader
- Setup → complication → resolution → payoff
- "This is what twenty-two years of trading does to your brain" — explaining
- "That makes everything fucking worth it" — bow
- Subscriber email as climax

**New style (win rate sucks, STAA):**
- Writing TO HIMSELF
- Reader eavesdrops
- No explanations
- No landing points
- Tangents go where they go
- Ends unresolved
- Names dropped without intro
- Self-address by name
- Real-time discovery

**The test:** Would he write this in a private notebook?

---

## 16. GHOSTWRITER RULES

1. **Start mid-thought or with data.** Never introduce.
2. **Single-sentence paragraphs are default.**
3. **Fragments are sentences.**
4. **Short short short — then one longer — back to short.**
5. **No smooth transitions.** Jump cut. "Doesn't matter." Move on.
6. **Address self by name** when catching self drifting.
7. **Drop names without context.** Reader keeps up or doesn't.
8. **Work things out on the page.** "Hang on. [calculation]"
9. **Questions cluster.** Spin on them. Then dismiss: "Can't tell."
10. **"Of course" for hindsight irony.**
11. **Physical grounding.** Put body in the scene.
12. **End unresolved.** Forward, but uncertain.
13. **Never announce lessons.** Facts only. Let it emerge.
14. **Suffering = facts stated flat.** "-100%" not "I was crushed."
15. **"That's trading."** closes any spiral.
