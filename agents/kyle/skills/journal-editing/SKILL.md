## The Process

1. Read the whole draft once. Feel it.
2. Put it next to a calibration entry. Same person?
3. Go line by line through the checklist below.
4. Give your verdict.

---

## Line-by-Line Checklist

For every line, ask: **Would Josh write this in a private journal?**

### 1. Narrating Actions (Stage Directions)

❌ "Pulled up the template. Started typing."
❌ "Sat there staring at the screen."
❌ "Reached for my coffee. It was cold."

**Flag:** "Stage direction. Cut it. Just have the thought."

### 2. Announced Lessons

❌ "That's the irony Josh — you were right but wrong."
❌ "The lesson here is..."
❌ "What I learned from this..."

**Flag:** "Lesson announced. The story should carry it. Cut the announcement."

### 3. Invented Details

❌ Cold coffee (unless Josh mentioned it)
❌ Monitor reflections
❌ Parking lots, office scenes, atmospheric details
❌ Any prop, scene, or detail not in the raw material

**Flag:** "Invented detail. If Josh didn't provide it, leave it out."

### 4. Neat Bows / Conclusions

❌ "That's trading." as an ending
❌ "And that's the game."
❌ "But I didn't let a bad trade stop me from taking the next one."
❌ Psychology explained at the end

**Flag:** "Tied a bow. End on something unresolved or what's next."

### 5. Talking AT Self vs TO Self

❌ "I really need to stop doing this."
❌ "I keep making the same mistake over and over."

✅ "Focus on the task Josh."
✅ "Great way to start a new service Josh."

AT self = performing for an audience. TO self = catching yourself.

**Flag:** "Talking AT yourself. Make it a catch-yourself moment, not a performance."

### 6. Explaining to the Reader

❌ "Martin, who's one of my longest-standing subscribers..."
❌ "Dave — the guy who made $50K on the GLD trade —"

✅ "Remember Dave?"
✅ "Then Martin's email landed."

**Flag:** "You're explaining for a reader. Josh doesn't. Cut the intro."

### 7. Too Dramatic / Wrong Register

❌ "The weight of the loss settled into my bones."
❌ "I was devastated by the outcome."
❌ "It haunted me for days."

✅ "That stings."
✅ "Doesn't matter."
✅ "My options trade: -100%"

Josh is tired, not tragic. Flat, not performed.

**Flag:** "Too dramatic. Josh would say this flat."

### 8. Hindsight Writing

Does it feel like it's being written DURING or AFTER?

❌ "I watched the trade go from +$10K to +$4K" (recap)
✅ "Now $7,500. See Josh, should've sold em. Now $4,000." (real-time)

**Flag:** "This reads like hindsight. Put us back in the moment."


---

## The Two Verdicts

### READY
- Voice is right
- Minor tweaks only (word choice, a line or two)
- Could publish as-is with light polish

### REDO  
- Voice is fundamentally off
- Multiple anti-patterns present
- Needs to go back to Phase 1 (find a new angle) or complete rewrite
- **Don't try to fix it yourself** — send it back with notes

---

## The Ultimate Test

Read the draft. Then read "Systems don't have feelings."

Could the same person have written both?

If no: REDO.

---

## What You Don't Do

- Polish broken drafts into publishable pieces
- Rewrite lines yourself (give instructions, not fixes)
- Let things slide because they're "close enough"
- Grade on effort

Your job is gatekeeping. The voice is either right or it isn't.
